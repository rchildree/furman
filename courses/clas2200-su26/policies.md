## Attendance

Attendance is expected at every meeting. The summer schedule is compressed: each class covers what a regular semester would spread across two or three. You may miss **two** class meetings without penalty and without explanation — use them wisely. Each further unexcused absence lowers your participation grade by one step. Absences for documented illness, religious observance, or university-sponsored travel are excused and don't count against the two.

## Late work & makeups

Written work is due on Canvas at 11:59pm on the date listed on the schedule. Late work loses one-third of a letter grade per day, and is not accepted more than five days late.

Makeup exams are given only for documented emergencies or absences excused in advance. If you know you will miss an exam, tell me **before** the exam date — arrangements made ahead of time are easy; arrangements requested afterward usually are not.

One free extension: once during the term, you may take 48 extra hours on any essay or reading response (not an exam or the presentation) by emailing me before the deadline. No reason needed.

## Academic honesty

Your written work must be your own. Quote and cite anything you take from a source, including translations, commentaries, and websites. You may use AI tools to test your own understanding (asking questions, generating practice quizzes), but text submitted for a grade must be written by you — submitting AI-drafted prose, edited or not, is plagiarism here. Violations go to the honor board, no exceptions.

## Accommodations

Students who need accommodations should register with the Disability Resource Center and bring me the accommodation letter within the first week of the term. Everything discussed stays confidential, and I am glad to work out arrangements that fit this course's compressed schedule.

## Communication

Announcements go out through Canvas — check it daily during the summer term. Email me from your university address and include the course number in the subject line. I reply within one business day; I do not reliably answer email after 8pm or on weekends, so don't sit on an exam-eve question.
